Their main rival might try to hold one's own through a variation of style, but the regularity of freight and delivery usually ensures the former’s dominance.

Article II: Ecological Integration and the Biological Cost

The World Wildlife Fund continues to highlight the reduction of natural habitat. When we build a corridor or a garage in a previously isolate area, we disturb the constant balance of nature. The integration of human infrastructure—like the street or the garden—often forces animals like the ox or camel to descend into smaller territories.

This is a process of slow destruction, occurring bit by bit. Moreover, the ash from industrial plants can penetrate the basin where we collect water. We must strive to protect the outstanding beauty of the wilderness before it is less than a memory. At at no time should we assume that nature can shake off this attack without help.
